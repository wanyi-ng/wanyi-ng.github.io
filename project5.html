ATypI 2020 All Over

Year
2021 (Spring semester)
Category
Conference Guide booklet
Overview
Final Project for Typography 2 class at CUNY City College of New York

Concept

Background

The Association Typographique Internationale, or ATypI, is an international and non-profit organization dedicated to typography, type design, and the type community. ATypI’s annual conferences aim to bring together diverse communities including typeface designers, lettering artists, publishers, developers, educators, and other members of the type, tech, and design communities.

ATypI held its sixty-fourth annual—and first virtual—conference from October 27, 2020 to October 31, 2020. The five-day and night event sought to inspire, engage, and entertain designers. Attendees were able to enjoy and participate in many fun workshops. Though the disadvantage of such virtual event is the lack of physical engagement an in-person experience would have provided, the virtual format permitted registered participants to view all presentations, interviews, and panels until November 15, 2020. Additionally, inclusivity and accessibility became more possible.

Color Palette

I noticed two promotional images of the event on ATypI's webpage. The background colors were similar to Yves Klein’s famous blue; one of the images had a gradient of green to the Yves-blue. The typefaces were a stencil sans-serif paired with a sans-serif in a silver color. I thought these combinations were quite bold, and a signature of the organization’s focus on type. However, I felt as I stared long, my eyes strained to read the text. I began to think about better color pairs that had similar vibrancies and saturation as the Klein blue, but would not cause the eye to strain with contrasting color text.

Font Pair

The typefaces used for this project were Apoc(alypse) from BlazeType and Suisse Int'l from Swiss Typefaces. According to BlazeType's website, the development of Apoc began with a book cover about the book of Revelations, in which the letters had a similar look to the Humanistic style, yet the creators were intrigued by the counter-forms. I was drawn by this origin story to use a font that had an archaic look paired with one that had a more modern, clean appearance to highlight the legacy of ATypI's conference. I wanted to honor their success in hosting annual conferences for so many years. Apoc and Suisse Int'l best represented the transition of times from the old to the modern era.

Process

I began with many sketches and variations of book covers and content page layouts (see below).

Some of initial concepts were having the conference title spaced out on the cover proportionally or randomly over a dot grid to symbolize social distancing. The implied squares would symbolize computer screens. Other concepts were enlarging "All Over" across the book cover (even cropping the phrase) or using repetition and/or a combination of various font styles for the letters in "all over" to illustrate text covering the entire cover.

Below are two sketches of layout concepts for each section (day of the week) of the conference. I thought a lot about how I wanted to lay the workshop times, titles, speakers, and descriptions, and how large I wanted each to be. Most importantly, I was attentative to how I could add texture to create hierarchy across the pages.

Final Version

For the final version, I decided to use the more vibrant and saturated colors on the cover to create a brighter mood and enlighten attendees during such period of hardship brought about by the pandemic. I wanted the viewer to be able to hold the conference guide and look forward to finding a workshop they were interested in, while feeling joy they could still come together with other type lovers across the world to celebrate type.

The cover has text laid diagonally to contrast the horizontal text layout of the content pages. Repetition of the conference name, the day of the week on each introduction section page, and large heading size was done to highlight the concept of text "all over" the page and focus on type.

In contrast to the cover, each section for each day of the conference had more muted colors to allow the text to be read comfortably (see below).
