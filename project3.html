Composure

Year
2019 (Fall semester)
Category
Collage, photo manipulation in Adobe PS
Overview
Self-portrait collage for 2D Imaging class at CUNY City College of New York

Concept

Composure is an experimentation project done in Photoshop. It is one of my most personal pieces; one of the reasons being including myself as a subject. It focuses on the internal anxiety one feels in social settings. "When someone looks at me, what do they see? What do they think of me?" Despite all our worries, we train ourselves to maintain an external composure.

Because of the masks and the walls we build to protect ourselves, the most peculiar thing about strangers in public places is the impossibility to fully know a person's thoughts, personality, interests, worries, and stories. Everyone has their own thoughts, battles, and stories.

I wanted to create a piece that would make the viewer question, "Is the one 'composed' maintaining composure as suggested by the title or the one internally breaking down losing composure, along with their control/ability to stabilize themselves in public, as depicted by the figure in the bottom right of the composition?

I was inspired by—and referenced—the lyrics in the song, "Noises", by Pale Waves and a moment in the music video when the lead singer, Heather Baron-Gracie, puts her hands up to her head in distress (see timestamp here):
What do you see when you look at me?//
I can't control my emotions lately//

Process

I began by preparing my assets. I isolated the image of Heather from the background by using the selection, wand, and masking tools. Then, I applied effects to the photograph of myself and now-edited image of Heather to give the effect of an X-ray scan to emphasize the feeling of being micro-observed and judged.
