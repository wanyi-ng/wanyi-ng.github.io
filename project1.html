This Is Me.

Year
2019 (Fall semester)
Category
Ink on paper, collage, photo manipulation in Adobe PS
Overview
Final project for 2D Imaging class at CUNY City College of New York

Concept

Create what you want to create
Who cares if someone says it’s unoriginal?
Create because your heart felt something
whether it was from your mind
or something you saw,
someone will see your passion and acknowledge that
I acknowledge that.

—Letter from me to you

This Is Me is a mini-series, a letter to critics and a letter for the viewer, and a promise to myself. It represents me breaking my silence as an artist and creator. I am not trying to come off as someone who is defensive, disrespectful, and unable to take criticism but rather acknowledging my worth and potential as an artist, standing up for my work, and showing confidence in the work that I produce. With this, I am able to continue creating work where no one can hinder my production and my growth to push forward, improve my skills, and make more work.

The series is a statement and promise to myself to always be who I am and create art that I love — for me that involves calligraphy. I am not trying to stand out nor have my own style. My style is a culmination of all the calligraphy forms that inspired me to pick up a pen and make the leap from “I want to do that, too” to teaching myself. Calligraphy has become a part of me. Sometimes I just want to sit down with a pen and ink and just write something—abstract or not, it’s art for art’s sake. This means my work sometimes does not have a deeper meaning the viewer must dissect; my goal is for the viewer to simply feel the curves and the sharp strokes of the calligraphic forms.

Process

This project combines calligraphy, which was done physically, and digital imagery. I obtained this image (above) of a fake heart from pxfuel.com and edited it in Adobe Photoshop. I isolated the heart from the background and an image of my hand (below).

I made two calligraphy pieces using mixed media paper and ink. Afterwards, I scanned each piece into a computer and edited each in Photoshop. Unlike the treatment of the photo of the heart and my hand, I did not isolate the calligraphy forms from their backgrounds. I had to carefully edit the lighting and backgrounds to create my canvas. It was especially difficult editing the piece with a white background because the paper was too large for the scanner I used, thus, I had to combined the scanned portions (see images below).

My letter forms are inspired by Pokras Lampas and Instagram user, @oscarcalligraphy—two calligraphers I study immensely and draw inspiration from when learning calligraphy.
